Australia's Major National Rail Upgrade: Inland Rail

1. The Rail Project

The project that matches all criteria is Inland Rail, a major national freight rail infrastructure project designed to create a 1,600-kilometre direct rail freight connection between Melbourne and Brisbane via regional Victoria, New South Wales, and Queensland [1][2]. The project involves 600 km of new track and 1,100 km of upgrades to existing rail lines [3].

2. Groundbreaking Date

Inland Rail broke ground on Thursday, 13 December 2018, at Parkes in the Central West of New South Wales. The official ceremony involved the turning of the first sod to mark the start of the first section of the project [4][5]. The Australian Government's media release confirmed: "Construction on the Inland Rail project has officially begun" on that date [5].

3. First/Augustal Section

The inaugural segment of Inland Rail was the Parkes to Narromine (P2N) section in central-western NSW. This was explicitly described as "the first of 13 sections on the Inland Rail Program to be completed" [6]. The project involved:

  • Upgrading 98.4 km of existing rail track
  • Constructing 5.3 km of new track near Parkes (known as the North West Connection)
  • Including a full rebuild of the rail corridor through several sites [7]

The section was completed on 15 September 2020, with the final golden clip hammered into place at Peak Hill [8][9].

4. Construction Consortium

The P2N section was built by the INLink Joint Venture (INLink JV), a partnership between:

  • BMD Constructions (an Australian-owned civil construction company)
  • Fulton Hogan (a New Zealand–headquartered international infrastructure group)

The contract was valued at approximately $310 million (some sources state $300 million) and was signed in October 2018 [4][10][11].

5. Motorway Connection

One half of the INLink JV — Fulton Hogan — is now also the lead construction consortium for a major motorway project:

Coomera Connector – Stage 1 Central

The Fulton Hogan-led consortium (formally known as the Fulton Hogan, JF Hull Holdings, and McIlwain Joint Venture — FHHMJV) won the main construction contract for the Coomera Connector Stage 1 Central package [12][13].

Key details:

  • Contract awarded: September 2024 (early works awarded January 2023) [13][14]
  • Project: Construction of an 8-kilometre, six-lane highway section between Helensvale Road and Smith Street Motorway [12]
  • Project value: Part of the broader Coomera Connector motorway, described as South East Queensland's largest road infrastructure project ($2.16 billion for Stage 1 alone, or up to $3.026 billion total) [14][15]
  • Nature: This is a brand-new motorway running parallel to the Pacific Motorway (M1) on the Gold Coast, Queensland

The Coomera Connector is being delivered in multiple packages:

  • Stage 1 North: ACCIONA (60%) and Georgiou Group (40%) JV [16]
  • Stage 1 Central: FHHMJV (Fulton Hogan, JF Hull, McIlwain) [12]
  • Stage 1 South: Seymour Whyte (per Infrastructure Pipeline) [17]

Identified Project

The Australian motorway project matching all criteria is the Coomera Connector (M9), located on the Gold Coast in Queensland. It was originally conceived during planning under the name "Intra Regional Transport Corridor" (often abbreviated as IRTC) [1][2]. History and Renaming

The corridor has been identified in various public planning documents since the 1990s [1]. A joint 2015 study between the Department of Transport and Main Roads (TMR) and the City of Gold Coast confirmed the corridor as a future strategic transport link to relieve traffic congestion on the Pacific Motorway [1]. The Stage 1 corridor was gazetted in 2016, with the remaining northern section gazetted between 2017 and 2019 [1]. The preferred route of the northern section was confirmed in April 2021 [1].

The project was known as the "Intra Regional Transport Corridor" throughout the planning and early design phases. Multiple Queensland Parliament documents confirm this transition in naming [2][7]. As early as 2018, then-Minister Mark Bailey referred to the corridor "now known as the 'Coomera Connector'" [7]. The TMR's own Right to Information release also explicitly states: "The Coomera Connector (formerly known as the Intra Regional Transport Corridor)" [8].

---

Investment and Funding

The federal and state governments initially committed a total of $1.53 billion on a 50:50 basis ($765.25 million each) to plan and construct Stage 1 [1][3]. This figure matches the user's description of "just over $1.5 billion."

However, by September 2021, media reported a cost blowout of approximately 40% ($632 million), bringing the total to approximately $2.1 billion [1]. By July 2024, the Albanese and Miles Labor Governments had each committed over $1.5 billion to the project, including an additional $432 million each from that year's budgets [6]. As of late 2025, the state government had contributed $1.9 billion and the federal government $1.5 billion [9].

---

Construction Status (as of Late 2025)

Stage 1 is a 16-kilometre motorway between Coomera and Nerang, delivered in three packages [1]:

  • Stage 1 North (Shipper Drive, Coomera to Helensvale Road, Helensvale): Opened 2 December 2025 — the first section of the Coomera Connector to open to traffic [1][6].
  • Stage 1 Central (Helensvale Road to Smith Street Motorway, Molendinar): Early works underway as of August 2024, with a main contract awarded in September 2024 to a consortium led by Fulton Hogan [1][10].
  • Stage 1 South (Smith Street Motorway, Molendinar to Nerang-Broadbeach Road, Nerang): A main construction tender with three preferred contractors was released in 2024 [10].

The overall 45-kilometre project (beyond just Stage 1) is only partially under active construction, with the other stages north of Coomera still in the planning phase as of 2021 [1]. The Coomera to Tugun (Coomera to the M1) connection, which is a different (and now completed) $1.5 billion project, is a separate but related M1 upgrade.
